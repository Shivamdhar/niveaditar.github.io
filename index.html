About the Author

Eleanor Voss

I write about the spaces between things — the pause before a word is spoken, the moment before a decision changes everything. My work explores ordinary lives cracked open by grief, desire, and the quiet persistence of hope.

My novels have been translated into fourteen languages and published across three continents. I've taught at the Iowa Writers' Workshop, the Bread Loaf Conference, and the Tin House Summer Workshop. I live in the Pacific Northwest with too many notebooks and not enough shelf space.

Before turning to fiction full-time, I was a journalist and briefly a cartographer — an occupation that, I've decided, was simply early training for the map-making that novels require.
