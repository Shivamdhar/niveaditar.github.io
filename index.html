About the Author

Niveadita Razdan

I’ve always been fascinated by the stories families tell themselves, the carefully edited versions of events that get passed down through generations, the silences that speak louder than words, the small betrayals and great loves that shape who we become. I write fiction that explores these hidden landscapes of memory and belonging.

My debut novel, The House on Gulmohar Lane, is my first published book, a culmination of all these themes I’ve been exploring in my shorter work. My fiction has also appeared in various publications and I regularly write essays about the craft of storytelling on Medium, where I explore everything from literary misdirection to the peculiar magic of late-night writing sessions. I believe the best stories don’t just entertain, they help us see ourselves and our world with a little more clarity, a little more compassion.

Before I became a full-time writer, I worked on projects that required a different kind of storytelling, data analysis, problem-solving, mapping vulnerabilities and patterns. That analytical background still influences how I approach character and plot: I’m interested in the architecture of story, in building narratives that reveal themselves slowly, layer by layer.

I write late at night, usually with tea going cold beside me. I believe in the power of imperfect first drafts, in the importance of getting words on the page even when they feel wrong. I’m endlessly curious about what makes a story work, what makes a character breathe, what makes a reader turn the page.
